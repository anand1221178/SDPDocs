User Feedback - Campus Safety App (Sibusiso, 17/09/2024)

  • Who controls the markers?
    • Sibusiso inquired about who controls the markers on the map, referencing how incidents and hotspots are managed.
  • How do the hotspots come about? Do people report incidents?
    • He asked if the app relies on user-reported incidents or if there’s another process for creating hotspots.
  • How do you prevent someone from spamming?
    • Concerned about spamming within the incident reporting feature, he asked how the app protects against abuse by malicious users.
  • Feedback on Interface:
    • Sibusiso mentioned that he liked the simplicity of the app's interface, appreciating the ease of use.
  • Protecting Against Malicious Users:
    • He suggested that the app should include protections against malicious users, such as bots that could flood the system with alerts or requests.
  • Verification of Alerts:
    • Sibusiso asked about how the authenticity of alerts and incidents is verified. Martin responded that users can post pictures on the platform as a way to verify incidents.

Summary of Stakeholder Feedback and Integration Discussions:

Stakeholder: Kabelo

  • Inquiry: Kabelo reached out to integrate a panic button feature into their application and requested more details on APIs, SDKs, and security guidelines.
  • Response: Luke mentioned that the panic button functionality was still in development, and a document detailing API endpoints had been shared but might change. Security specifics were not yet available.
  • Follow-up: Kabelo later sought clarification on the difference between emergency alerts and incident reporting. Luke explained that the Alerts API broadcasts real-time emergency threats, while the Incidents API tracks and reports specific safety incidents.
  • Outcome: Kabelo showed interest in using the Alerts API for events after understanding that alerts are used for immediate threats, while incidents cover follow-ups.
